Logistic Regression Analysis

I pulled the price data for LCID stock since going public, and aggregated the data such that each experimental unit had a response variable of closing price of LCID on each day, and predictor variables were closing price on each of the 5 days preceding the current day. I then implemented a binary coding of each variable:
  • 0 - meaning the price of that day is lower than the previous day
  • 1 - meaning the price of that day is higher than the previous day
My final logistic model thus had 4 predictors & 1 response variable I found the model had a 58% True Negative Accuracy on both the training data and the entire dataset. However, the model poorly predicted Positives. If one were to make decisions based on this model, a Negative prediction is certainly more confident than a Positive prediction. Perhaps shorting the stock on such predictions is the play.
